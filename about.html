About Us

We saw the shift before
the denials did.

For a decade, molecular testing grew by adding more targets to every panel. Payers have made it clear that detection alone isn't enough — and most labs can't document why a given test was ordered for a given case. Oriole Sciences was built to answer that question, before the claim is ever at risk.

The Market Shift

A market under pressure.

The molecular testing industry spent the last decade building bigger panels on the assumption that more targets meant more value — more data, more reimbursement, more advantage.

Payers stopped agreeing. Carriers are now asking a question most labs can't answer: why was this specific test ordered for this specific case? Without a documented, defensible answer, denials follow — and in 2026, they've climbed sharply.

Nearly two years ago, working across a range of laboratories, providers, and revenue-cycle teams, we recognized this shift before most of the market did. Broad, undifferentiated testing strategies were running out of runway. That recognition is why Oriole Sciences exists: an intelligence and infrastructure layer built specifically to answer what should be ordered, for whom, and why it holds up — operationally, financially, and on documentation.

Our Origin

From lab infrastructure to the intelligence layer around it.

Oriole Sciences started by solving the operational side of molecular testing: building labs, validating workflows, and fixing reimbursement. That work surfaced a bigger opportunity — most of the value in molecular testing doesn't live in the assay itself.

It lives in the system that decides which sourced assay to order, why it's defensible for this case, how the result gets documented, and how the claim gets paid.

Not the assay science — that belongs to the manufacturers who develop and scientifically validate it.

Oriole Sciences owns everything around it — the data, the documentation logic, the reimbursement strategy, and the commercial engine that brings it to market.

What We've Built

The Oriole Sciences Intelligence Layer

We've built a growing set of proprietary tools that improve the decisions surrounding every sourced assay we help deploy:

01 — Prevalence Intelligence

Which panels are actually relevant.

Turns laboratory data into a clear answer: which sourced panels are relevant for a given population, region, and care setting — replacing habit and vendor catalogs with real prevalence data.

02 — Medical Necessity Intelligence

Defensibility before the order is placed.

Connects case presentation, risk factors, and ICD-10 logic before an order is placed — documenting medical necessity at the point of ordering instead of discovering the gap during a denial.

03 — Reimbursement Intelligence

Claim defense moved upstream.

A living map of payer policy, documentation requirements, coding constraints, and denial patterns — moving claim defense upstream instead of treating it as a billing afterthought.

04 — Workflow & Reporting Intelligence

Quality assurance above the instrument.

Standardizes how run quality is checked and results are reported — reducing manual review, improving reproducibility, and giving every lab a scalable quality assurance layer.

05 — Commercial Intelligence

Scale beyond what a sales team can reach.

An AI-assisted engine for outreach, education, and business development — built to scale adoption beyond what a traditional sales team can reach alone.

Each program strengthens the next. Every lab, every case, and every claim outcome adds information back into the system — so the intelligence layer gets sharper the more it's used.
