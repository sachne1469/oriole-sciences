Payer Strategy

Payer Strategy.
The standard. Not a slogan.

An operational and financial standard for molecular lab programs — built to hold up when payers, auditors, and regulators ask why. What the industry calls Defensible PCR, we call the minimum standard.

Why It Exists

The market created the problem. We built the answer.

Payers are no longer reimbursing broad molecular programs without documented justification. Labs built around volume — not defensibility — are facing skyrocketing denial rates.

Defensible PCR is the operational standard that answers the question payers are already asking: why this panel, for this patient, in this setting?

How It Works
Operationally

Built from day one.

Workflow validated, throughput documented, and audit-ready documentation in place from standup. Not a checklist completed under audit pressure — a system built to operate this way permanently.

Financially

Upstream. Not reactive.

Reimbursement infrastructure built upstream — denial patterns addressed before claims are at risk. 835 triage, CPT/ICD-10 alignment, and payer-specific documentation requirements mapped before the first claim goes out.

Regulatorily

Documentation by design.

Documentation discipline embedded from standup — not retrofitted after the audit arrives. Structured medical necessity documentation, chain-of-custody protocols, and QC records built to survive scrutiny.
